Footnotes

Some Source Texts have footnotes, and BTT-Writer can help you to translate the footnotes.

Also, sometimes a translator will want to add a footnote to explain something that is difficult to translate. #### A Quick Short-cut If making footnotes is too complicated, but the translators want to have them, we suggest using [[] ]] to set the footnote off from the rest of the text. In this case, that would be >[!note] :raw-latex:`\v 28` But if someone says to you, “This has been offered in sacrifice,” then do not eat it, both for the sake of the one who informed you, and for the sake of conscience— [[Some ancient copies of the Greek text add, For the earth and everything in it belong to the Lord. But the best ancient copies of the Greek text do not have this. Many scholars see this addition as a duplication of verse 26.]] #### Footnotes in USFM We have mentioned before that BTT-Writer (and several other programs) use USFM to format the text.

USFM is not concerned with Bold, Italic, Underline, and other formats like that. Instead, it deals with what the content of the text is. - :raw-latex:`\v 1` means that the content following is the first verse of a chapter. - :raw-latex:`\f means` that the following text is a footnote. The footnote continues until it reaches :raw-latex:`\f*.` - :raw-latex:`\ft `begins the text of the footnote. :raw-latex:`fqa and :raw-latex:fqa* `bracket a quotation in the footnote.

With this knowledge, we can format the passage like this: >[!note] :raw-latex:`\v 28` But if someone says to you, “This has been offered in sacrifice,” then do not eat it, both for the sake of the one who informed you, and for the sake of conscience— :raw-latex:`\f +` :raw-latex:`\ft `Some ancient copies of the Greek text add,  fqa For the earth and everything in it belong to the Lord :raw-latex:`fqa`* . But the best ancient copies of the Greek text do not have this. Many scholars see this addition as a duplication of verse 26. :raw-latex:`\f\*`

Once a footnote has been added to the text, it will look strange in BTT-Writer, unless it is re-imported as a source text. (This almost never happens.) However, you can display the footnote when the translation is printed.
